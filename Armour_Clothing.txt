# Clothing System
# Version: 2026-03-16
# Non-armour layers for Calradia RP. These provide zero AC but handle cosmetics, social signalling, weather protection, and minor situational bonuses. They layer freely over any armour tier without mechanical conflict.
# Two tiers only:
# - None-tier: Base layer worn directly against skin. Never overlaps with other None-tier items.
# - Outdoor-tier: Outer garments worn over armour or None-tier. Highly variable and stackable.
# All clothing uses the Universal Armour Upgrade Components Table where applicable (e.g., Insulated or Breathable upgrades).
# Coverage Rule: Clothing has no mechanical coverage % — it is purely visual and situational.

## Clothing Interpretation Rules
Parse the item title for tier and keywords to determine Enc and situational effects.  
- None-tier items are always 100% coverage on their body area (underclothes).  
- Outdoor-tier items have variable coverage based on style (e.g., full cloak = full body, short surcoat = torso only).  
Base Enc is low. Upgrades from the Universal Table can be applied if material matches (textile/padded/leather).  
Craftsmanship applies as global (+1 Fellowship or +10 Concealment for Good/Best).  
Situational modifiers in Notes (warmth, social, concealment, etc.).

## Cultural Notes (How Each Culture Uses Clothing)
| Culture     | Typical Preferences & Adaptations |
|-------------|-----------------------------------|
| Vlandia     | Bold heraldic surcoats and tabards over armour are extremely common. Fur-lined mantles and heavy cloaks are favoured in the north. Clean linen under-layers are a mark of status. |
| Empire      | Formal red or gold-trimmed cloaks and embroidered robes are preferred for court and legion service. Silk base layers are common among officers. Practical and disciplined appearance. |
| Battania    | Hooded cloaks with woad or leaf patterns for woodland concealment. Wool tunics and natural dyes are standard. Fur mantles are rare and usually reserved for chieftains. |
| Sturgia     | Heavy fur-lined mantles and thick wool tunics are everyday wear. Dark, rugged cloaks with bear or wolf trim are common. Clothing is built for extreme cold. |
| Khuzait     | Light, flowing cloaks and silk/linen robes for mobility on horseback. Bright colours and horse motifs are popular. Breathable base layers are essential in the steppe heat. |
| Aserai      | Loose, flowing robes and burnous-style cloaks for desert travel. Silk base layers and embroidered outer garments are status symbols. Emphasis on heat resistance and ventilation. |
