Rigid Partial Torso Interpretation Rules
Parse the item title for type and component keywords. All torso items have a hard cap due to rigid plates and articulation gaps:

Coat of Plates: 80% Torso Coverage by default
Laminar and Cuir Bouilli: 60% Torso Coverage by default

Component-to-Location Tag Mapping
Coat of Plates (80% Torso Cap)

Front Plate: (Front Upper)(Front Lower) – 35%
Back Plate: (Back) – 35%
Transverse Top Plate: (Front Upper)(Back) – 10%
Segmented Shoulders: (Shoulder) – +10% to Arms when present

Laminar & Cuir Bouilli (60% Torso Cap)

Front Plate: (Front Upper)(Front Lower) – 25%
Back Plate: (Back) – 25%
Yoke Plates: (Gorget)(Shoulders) – 10%
Arm coverage from Yoke Plates: +10% to Arms when present

Tassets (separate module – hangs and floats over thighs)

Short Tassets: (Tasset) – 20%
Long Tassets: (Tasset) – 40%

AC applies proportionally to covered areas. For any hit, roll d100 ≤ the component’s coverage % to apply AC; failure = gap.
Base Enc from type; +4 Enc per additional component. Round to nearest 5.
Upgrades from the Universal Table may be applied if the material matches.
Craftsmanship global (+1/+2 AC); no Dodge bonus.

Boots Integration Note
Boots are handled in boots_armour.txt. Cuir Bouilli / Splinting = Rigid Partial tier; Greaves = Rigid Uniform tier. Coverage is additive with Cuisses and Tassets.

Core Rules

Rigid Partial = segmented plates or bands with visible joints.
All helmets have a 95% Head Coverage hard cap.
The final 5% is always the visor zone (0 AC unless a Visor or Full Visor upgrade is installed).
Upgrades follow Location Tags: only one Face upgrade and one Neck upgrade may be combined.
