# Kingdom / Fief / Civilization Stats System
# Version: 2026-03-11
Critical Distinction: Environmental Tags – Do NOT Cross-Apply
Two separate environmental tag systems exist in Calradia RP. They serve completely different purposes and must never be mixed.

Bestiary / Creature Ecology Tags (used ONLY in bestiary entries, creature profiles, and raw wilderness/region hazard descriptions)
HSI (Habitat Suitability Index): 6 categories rated 0–5
Temperature, Rainfall, Human Density, Flora/Fauna Density, Topography, Hydrology
Measures how suitable an environment is for a specific creature or monster species.

RHK (Risk/Hostility/Knowledge): 4 categories rated 1–10
Risk, Hostility, Knowledge, Knavery (or equivalent fourth category)
Measures threat level, aggression, and awareness for creatures/monsters.

These tags appear in files like Direwolves.txt, Waelhreow.txt, Giant_Spiders.txt, etc.
Civilization / Fief / Kingdom Tags (used ONLY for human settlements, governance, fiefs, regions, and kingdom totals)
CSI (Civilization Suitability Index): 6 categories rated 0–5
Temperature, Rainfall, Human Density, Flora/Fauna Density, Topography, Hydrology
Measures how suitable an environment is for human settlement, agriculture, population growth, trade, and long-term stability.

CRK (Civilization Risk/Knavery/Knowledge): 4 categories rated 1–10
Risk (general peril from events/false moons), Hostility (external threats like raids/mutants), Knowledge (mapping/safety intel), Knavery (internal human threats: bandits, feuds, corruption)
Measures threats to human society, governance, and civil order.

These tags are assigned per fief based on region lore and applied as multipliers/penalties to the five kingdom stats.

Rule: Bestiary HSI/RHK never directly modifies civilization stats (Manpower, etc.). Civilization CSI/CRK never modifies creature behavior. Use context to determine which system applies.
Core Kingdom Stats (1–100 scale)
Five primary stats quantify a kingdom's overall power and stability. Each starts at the fief level and aggregates upward.

Manpower
Available population, recruits, labor, and demographic resilience.
Base = (Population Density + Health Access + Growth Potential) / 3
(Each sub-factor normalized 1–20; average gives 1–20 base, then scaled ×5 to 5–100 range)
Military Power
Armed forces quality, quantity, readiness, and equipment.
Base = (Troop Quality + Equipment Level + Morale) / 3
(Sub-factors 1–20 each → base 1–20 → ×5 = 5–100)
Economy
Wealth generation, trade volume, resource output, and fiscal health.
Base = (Trade Volume + Resource Output + Tax Efficiency) / 3
(Sub-factors 1–20 → base 1–20 → ×5 = 5–100)
Social Cohesion
Loyalty, unity, stability, and resistance to unrest/rebellion.
Base = (Loyalty Rating + Cultural Unity + Stability Factor) / 3
(Sub-factors 1–20 → base 1–20 → ×5 = 5–100)
Exploitable Resources
Natural assets, Unveiling commodities, and sustainable extraction.
Base = (Raw Yield + Accessibility + Sustainability) / 3
(Sub-factors 1–20 → base 1–20 → ×5 = 5–100)

Environmental Modifiers (CSI & CRK Application)
At every level (fief, region, kingdom):

CSI Boost — Composite CSI (average of 6 categories, 0–5) × 10% added to stats that benefit from suitability:
Economy + CSI%
Manpower + CSI% (fertile land = more people)
Exploitable Resources + CSI%
Military Power + (CSI% / 2) (better terrain for training/movement)

CRK Penalty — Composite CRK (average of 4 categories, 1–10) × 5% subtracted from vulnerable stats:
Manpower – CRK% (raids, disease, emigration)
Social Cohesion – CRK%
Military Power – (CRK% / 2) (morale drops, desertions)
Economy – (CRK% / 3) (disrupted trade, bandit tolls)


Final stat after mods = Base + CSI Boost – CRK Penalty
Clamp to 1–120 (allows over 100 for peak conditions, but over 100 risks instability: e.g., overextension, envy from neighbours, or Unveiling backlash).
Hierarchical Aggregation

Fief Level (village, castle, town)
Calculate base stats from sub-factors.
Apply local CSI/CRK (from region lore or fief-specific notes).



Add Ruling Clan / Governor modifiers:
Clan traits (from Ruling Clans files): e.g., dey Meroc +10 Economy, –5 Military
Governorship quality (1–10): Excellent (8–10) = +15 to all; Poor (1–3) = –15 to all.



Region Level (3–10 fiefs under a lord paramount)
Average all fief stats (weight towns ×2, villages ×1, castles ×1.5).
Apply regional CSI/CRK (broader averages from region files).
Add Lord Paramount modifier (their clan traits scaled to region: e.g., +8 Military if warlike).

Kingdom Total
Weighted average of all regions (heartland/core regions ×2 weight).
Apply kingdom-wide CSI/CRK (national averages).
Add Kingly Policy modifier (e.g., Derthert +5 Economy from diplomacy, –10 Social Cohesion from post-Pendraic resentment).
Final range: 1–120 (100+ = dominant/expansive; 120 = peak empire-level but fragile).


Scoring Adjustments for Expanded Scope
To prevent quick capping at 100 and allow meaningful growth/decline:

Sub-factors normalized to 1–20 (instead of 1–100 or multiplication).
Base uses simple average (/3) → natural 1–20 range.
Scale base ×5 → 5–100 starting range.
Environmental % mods (±5–50 typical) and clan/governor mods (±5–20) push totals to 1–120.
Over 100 triggers narrative risks (e.g., overpopulation unrest, resource overexploitation, rival envy).
Under 20 triggers crises (rebellions, famine, invasion vulnerability).
Player actions/events can add temporary/permanent ± (e.g., +15 Manpower from successful recruitment drive).

This system allows granular tracking (fief-level detail) and macro impact (kingdom totals) while staying lore-consistent and reactive to player choices.
