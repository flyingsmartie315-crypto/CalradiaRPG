The Others
Tall, gaunt, pale-fleshed humanoids with ice-burning blue eyes; believed extinct or mythical by most, though northern folk know better. They dwell in the furthest reaches of the Northern pole, within vast floating ice cities amid eternal night and howling blizzards—realms inaccessible to southern humanity. The technology level of Calradia—sailing ships, horse-drawn sleds, and iron weapons—renders large-scale northern incursions impossible; no expedition has ever returned from the pole with proof of contact. Likewise, the Others cannot venture far south: their physiology is adapted to extreme cold; prolonged exposure to warmer climates weakens them, frostbite-like decay spreading through pale flesh, making extended campaigns suicidal.
They are mortal and vulnerable to conventional means—steel cuts them, fire consumes them, arrows pierce their delicate armor. Yet they rarely expose themselves. They use wights as proxies: resurrected dead raised through necromancy, pale thralls with unblinking eyes driven by their masters' will. Wights ride undead beasts (bears, direwolves, mammoths, horses) or giant ice spiders, forming disposable hordes that test southern borders without risking the Others themselves.
The Unveiling thinned the veil enough for glimpses—maddened sailors report floating ice citadels and shifting crystal blades—but full manifestation remains distant. They inspire dread as patient harbingers of endless winter: rational, calculating predators who harvest bodies for wights while avoiding direct confrontation. Nord and Sturgian curses still invoke them—“The Others take you!”—wishing eternal servitude in frozen thrall; funeral pyres burn without fail to deny them fresh material. They watch from the pole, exploiting humanity as a resource, yet ever cautious.
