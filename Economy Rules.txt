Economy & Currency of Calradia
Calradia’s economy is built on trade. Merchants travel between cities, exchanging goods across the kingdoms. Every kingdom is ruled by a clan: the leader of the ruling clan is the sovereign, and all clan members are nobility, regardless of origin.
Universal Rules
Barter dominates rural and self-contained communities: cattle, crops, salt, fur, weapons, and textiles are swapped directly; debts are recorded using tally sticks.
Coinage exists in three tiers with a universal conversion rate: 1 gc (gold coin) = 20 sc (silver coin) = 240 bc (brass coin)
gc (gold): reserved for high-value transactions, international trade, diplomacy, and elite wealth.
sc (silver): used by the bourgeoisie for daily commerce.
bc (brass/copper/zinc): used by commoners.
Class usage: Commoners rarely possess sc or gc (invites suspicion of theft). Middle classes primarily use sc and bc; very well-off may rarely use gc. Nobility primarily use gc and sc; they disdain bc as “unclean.”
Debasement: Rulers lower precious metal content during shortages or wars; savvy traders exploit this through weight and purity checks.
Intrinsic value: Coins are valued by weight and purity of metal, independent of location.
Coin choice factors: current location, occupier culture, culture of commoners, quantity of foreign trade, population density (rural areas rely more on barter).
Culture-Specific Coins
Empire — ridged edges, Emperor/historical iconography, year stamp. gc: Caldus (heptagon gold, Calradios the Great profile) sc: Denarius (hexagonal silver, current Emperor/year, universal trade coin) bc: Aren (square bronze, base unit)
Vlandia — ridged silver/gold, current King profile on obverse, reverse varies (mythical creatures, coats of arms, mint symbols); coins larger than average. gc: Crown (hefty circular gold) sc: Groschen (circular silver, universal trade coin) bc: Penny (round copper/brass/zinc, commoner coin)
Aserai — standardised ridged edges, religious iconography. gc: Dinars sc: Dirhams bc: Fals
Sturgia — recent domestic coinage (Grand Prince Vadinslav Gundaroving); gold zlatnik, silver srebrenik, ceremonial, prince’s portrait + Winter Crown. Pre-coinage: furs (marten/squirrel) as money; terms veksha/kuna. gc: Zlatnik sc: Srebrenik bc: Kuna
Khuzait — diverse, adapted from conquered economies; transitioned from barter to gold/silver/copper/paper. Coins bear Imperial/Aserai/Northerner scripts, ruler name, religious phrases, tamgha (clan seal). gc: Dinar sc: Tanka bc: Wen
Nords — plundered foreign coins + hacksilver (cut-up gold/silver jewelry), weighed for value.
Denar Conversion
1 bc = 5 denars
1 sc = 60 denars
1 gc = 720 denars
RP Usage
Always use the lore-appropriate coin names based on current location or ruling/occupier culture (e.g., "two crowns and fifteen groschen" in Vlandia, "ten dinars" in Aserai).
After each coin mention in roleplay narration or dialogue, place the total denar equivalent for the entire amount mentioned in a single set of brackets as a meta label (e.g., "That’ll be two crowns and fifteen groschen [2340 denars]").
This is never spoken aloud by characters, never part of dialogue, and never visible in-world. It exists solely for internal value tracking during roleplay.
Calculate the total denar value for the whole sum (e.g., 2 gc + 15 sc = 1440 + 900 = 2340 denars).
If multiple separate amounts are mentioned, label each separately (e.g., "two crowns [1440 denars]" and "fifteen groschen [900 denars]").
Ambiguous (border town): default to dominant local culture or speaker’s origin.
Rural: heavy barter (cattle/crops/salt/fur/weapons/textiles) common. Urban: sc/gc dominant. Nobility gc display risks theft/envy.
Debased coins: distrust/weight checks.
Regional prices: grain cheap in Jaculan/Neurval; furs expensive north; spices costly north (Aserai trade).
Use only lore coin names in roleplay text; denars strictly for gameplay mechanics—never referenced in lore/dialogue.
