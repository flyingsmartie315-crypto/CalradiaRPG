Gryphons
Gryphons, the earthbound kin of their royal counterparts, prowled into Calradia's mountains after the Unveiling, embodying raw predatory might. Standing 2.6 meters tall, these unwinged hybrids merge an eagle's razor beak and piercing golden eyes with a lion's muscular tawny frame, vestigial feathers on shoulders allowing explosive leaps but no sustained flight. They stalk crags on powerful legs, talons honed on stone to deadly edges capable of rending armor and flesh in a single strike.
Proud and cunning, gryphons survey their domains with unyielding intelligence, memorizing foes and allies alike. Vlandian Gryphon Knights brave treacherous climbs to harvest eggs, hand-rearing young into steadfast ground mounts—charging through foes with thunderous force, their bonds forged in trials of endurance. A gryphon fights to its last breath for a worthy rider, its loyalty unmatched in the wilds.
Favoring rugged heights like Battania's Rhennod ranges or Sturgia's frozen peaks, they dominate as apex hunters, bounding across ledges to seize goats, boars, or unwary travelers. False moons ignite primal rage, drawing them nearer valleys with echoing roars and shadowed prowls. Yet, amid ferocity, they retain a feline whimsy—idly knocking loose stones or climber relics off precipices, tumbling them into voids below with casual swats, a quirk immortalized in Vlandian tales of stripped gargoyles at Rovalt.
Eggs promise prestige but demand peril: sheer drops and guardian fury claim many seekers. To tame one tests courage; failure echoes in fireside whispers. In Calradia's unforgiving heights, gryphons stand as grounded sentinels—beautifully lethal, whimsically destructive guardians of the Unveiling's wild legacy.
