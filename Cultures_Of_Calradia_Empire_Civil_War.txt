Cultures of Calradia: Empire Civil War
Overview
The Calradic Empire's civil war, erupting in 1084 AE after Emperor Arenicos Pethros' assassination, fractured the once-unified realm into three rival factions: the Northern Empire (Senate-led oligarchs), the Southern Empire (aristocratic traditionalists), and the Western Empire (military populists). This conflict pits ideological visions against each other—senatorial rule vs. hereditary monarchy vs. army-elected leadership—amid the Unveiling's chaos, where false moons summon spectral legions and thin the veil in ruined battlefields. Shared imperial culture persists in fragments: disciplined legions, Latin-inspired tongue, and the Imperial Rite, but each faction adapts it to their agenda. The war has ravaged central Calradia, with Themes (military districts) contested, economies strained, and opportunistic neighbors like Vlandia and Battania encroaching. Veterans form mercenary bands like the Legion of the Betrayed, while spies manipulate shadows. The Empire's fate hangs on who claims the Dragon Banner, but reunification seems distant as grudges deepen.
Northern Empire
The Northern Empire stands as the embodiment of the oligarchic Senate, which flocked to Senator Lucon Osticos after Garios Comnos and Rhagaea Pethros laid claim on the title of Emperor with their respective ties in the military and the aristocracy. The bulk of this faction's clans are purportedly ancient lineages with long-held roots in the Senate - and thus this third of the Empire is more traditionalistic and conservative in nature, favoring the supremacy of the Senate over the Emperor and fearing the civil war rift that testifies to the bloated power of the Emperor. The Senate asserts that the Empire's decline is a consequence of alarmingly powerful Emperors who are not held accountable by their electors, and views Garios and Rhagaea as consequences of these monarchic trends. In reference to the old ways of the Empire, Lucon states that "without [our] law, without the written word, there is but tyranny and the mob."
Lucon Osticos
Age: 62 (born 1022 AE)
Titles: Emperor of the Northern Empire, Leader of Clan Osticos, Claimant to the Imperial Diadem
Lucon was serving as a junior officer in the staff of Emperor Drosios Neretzes. Following the defeat at Pendraic, he became a member of the Calradic Senate and gained great influence. When Neretzes' successor, Arenicos Pethros, was suspiciously murdered, Lucon became the leader of a group of powerful senators who claimed the Senate should take the role of ruling the Empire. Lucon's claim is based on the old Imperial law that the Senate should elect the person for the role.
Personality & Reputation
Cunning diplomat with a stern, scholarly presence; values law and tradition, but accused of oligarchic puppetry.
Politics
Lucon favors a return to an oligarchic Senate, and is politically opposite to the militant populism of Garios, while fundamentally opposed to the hereditary monarchy of Rhagaea, his two imperial contenders. Lucon sympathized with Drosios Neretzes and his handling of the Battle of Pendraic — recalling the desperate circumstances the Calradic Empire was in at the time. Though he begrudgingly looks on Arenicos Pethros for his politics, he admits transparently that his reign was the stabilizing force that the Empire needed at that time. As the Emperor was assassinated just before selecting his heir, the succession dispute saw the Empire's military fall in for Garios, and its southern landholders and aristocracy throw their weight for Rhagaea, the Emperor's widow. Thus, the Senate elected Lucon as Emperor, despite the military and aristocracy separately raising their own claimants, drawing the border for the three-way civil war that would follow.
Claim to the Throne
Lucon's claim to unify Calradia as Emperor relies on him holding the support of what remains of the Imperial Senate. Because of their support, Lucon asserts that he is the only one with legal precedent to be Emperor. How exactly the Senate supports his claim is still unknown, as is the traditional method of Imperial succession, but it is expected to be further developed as the game advances. Lucon's claim to unify Calradia as Emperor relies on his popular backing of the Imperial Senate. Because of their support, Lucon asserts that his election as Emperor proves that he is the only legal claimant to the title. As the last and only two Emperors (that we know of thus far) were elected by the Senate, the claim has won over the backing of the more ancient and prestigious clans of the Empire. Perhaps most notably of all, Lucon vows to do away with the title of Emperor once the war is won, and instead permanently reinstate the Senate as the primary authority over the Empire — a decision perhaps correlated to the strong backing he has won with the most influential senators.
Strengths and Weaknesses
Strength: Strong diplomatic and administrative networks, leveraging ancient senatorial alliances to secure funding from wealthy landowners and hire reliable mercenaries, allowing for sustained campaigns without over-relying on levies. Lucon's deep knowledge of the Empire's laws and traditions enables masterful legal maneuvering, outflanking rivals through precedent and alliances.
Weakness: Internal gridlock from senatorial debates often delays decisions, leading to slow mobilizations and vulnerability to decisive strikes from more unified foes.
Southern Empire
Ruled by Rhagaea Pethros, who claims the throne for Ira, hers and the late Emperor Arenicos' daughter and only child, the Southern Empire shares a border with the Aserai, the Khuzait, and the two other splinter states resulting from the fracturing of the Calradic Empire (Northern and Western). This faction emphasizes aristocratic traditions and hereditary rule, viewing the civil war as an opportunity to establish a stable monarchy free from senatorial interference or populist upheavals. Their holdings in the southern basins foster close ties with Aserai merchants, blending imperial stoicism with southern opulence in lavish courts and cultic reverence for the imperial family.
Rhagaea Pethros
Age: 42 (born 1042 AE)
Titles: Empress of the Southern Empire, Leader of Clan Pethros, Claimant to the Imperial Diadem
Rhagaea was the wife of the late Emperor Arenicos, who was suspiciously murdered. As his widow and mother to his daughter Ira, who is now heir-apparent, Rhagaea claims it is the right of the Emperor's family to inherit the Empire, and some Imperial citizens have also chosen to support her in the belief that a hereditary succession is what the Empire needs. It is worth noting that, despite her age, Rhagaea’s widowhood only enhanced her position and not only in politics. She soon became the most sought-after woman on the entire continent. Suitors from every realm relentlessly attempt to win her favor, yet she has never tired of dismissing them, remaining resolute and unmoved by their advances. Despite the fact that she leads a noble clan of the Empire, she was born a daughter to the Darshi Shah.
Personality & Reputation
Charismatic and ambitious, blending mercy with ruthless politics; elegant and resolute, admired for her strength but courted endlessly.
Politics
Rhagaea favors a hereditary succession over the Senate elections, with a more feudal system of absolute governance under the Emperor. Rhagaea believes politics and history is defined by the law of succession — this stance likely derives from both her origin as daughter to the Darshi Shah and the sentiment among the Southern nobility, only uttered in hushed tones prior to Arenicos' assassination. The civil war is a result of a fundamental archaic law that prevent her and her daugher Ira from succeeding the popular Emperor Arenicos because of their gender.
Connecting these dots may lead one to believe that Rhagaea's ambition lay at the heart of Arenicos' death. His demise allowed her, a foreign princess, to ascend to the imperial throne and bind the commonfolk to her cause, albeit at the cost of angering the Vaegir Guard. By placing the blame upon the Guard, she successfully unified the masses with promises of vengeance for her slain husband.
It is also worth considering that the southern clans have long harbored ambitions of establishing hereditary rule, in direct opposition to Arenicos' and Lucon’s belief in senatorial elections. Removing Arenicos from the political landscape enabled Rhagaea to assume an active role in grooming her daughter, Ira, not merely as heir to the Pethros clan, but as a future ruler of the Empire itself. Regarded as too wild for her noble station, Ira represents a departure from the path Arenicos envisioned for her—one Rhagaea appears determined to correct.
Claim to the Throne
Rhagaea's claim to unify Calradia as Empress is highly controversial and in part carries responsibility for sparking the civil-war. She claims that the Empire should pass on to her and later on to Ira when she's ready as they carry on the lineage of the late Emperor Arenicos. Despite these facts, until now and for many generations succession was decided in the halls of the Senate where the emperors are elected by the nobility of the Empire. She blames this system for weakening the Calradia and allowing weaker claimants like Lucon Osticos to ascend the throne.
Strengths and Weaknesses
Strength: Elite, loyal cataphract-heavy forces bolstered by Aserai influences, excelling in sieges and defensive warfare, with a stable command structure rooted in noble bloodlines that minimizes internal betrayal. Rhagaea’s cleverness (or cunning) allows masterful intrigue, outmaneuvering foes through alliances and blame-shifting.
Weakness: Elitism alienates common soldiers and populists, causing recruitment shortages and resentment, while overemphasis on heredity risks incompetent leadership if heirs like Ira prove unready.
Ira Pethros
Young noblewoman and heir-apparent to the Southern Empire, daughter of Arenicos and Rhagaea. Brave and calculating, she is groomed as the future empress to secure the faction's vision of monarchical stability.
Western Empire
Commanded by General Garios Comnos from Jalmarys, the Western Empire embodies populist militarism, demanding the army choose rulers through assemblies of veterans. Controlling western coasts like Zeonica and Onica Castle, their culture prioritizes merit, land grants for soldiers, and disdain for aristocrats. Legions are veteran-heavy, adaptable with adopted tactics like crossbows. False moons rally troops with spectral warriors, seen as calls to reform.
Emperor Garios Comnos
Age: 43 (born 1041 AE)
Titles: Emperor of the Western Empire, Leader of Clan Comnos, Claimant to the Imperial Diadem
Broad-shouldered and battle-scarred, Garios has the weathered face of a lifelong soldier and the commanding presence of a man who has led armies through fire. He rose from the ranks under Emperor Drosios Neretzes to become one of the most popular generals in the Empire. After the suspicious murder of Emperor Arenicos, his soldiers proclaimed him Emperor in a grand assembly, sparking the three-way civil war against Lucon Osticos and Rhagaea Pethros.
Personality & Reputation
Charismatic voice rallies troops; generous to allies, devious in plots, daring in field, cruel to foes, calculating in court. Loyal to friends but manipulative.
Politics
Garios represents the populist faction of the Calradic Empire, and has the backing of most of the former Empire's more esteemed soldiers and commanders. During the waning days of the Empire, he allegedly pushed for key reforms such as veteran land grants and popular trial by assembly. As these policies were likely blocked or gridlocked by the oligarchic forces of the Senate and feudal influences of the aristocracy and former Emperors, his ascendancy as claimant for Emperor is likely a combination of his popularity with the Imperial armies and his populist political agenda.
Claim to the Throne
Garios's claim to unify Calradia as Emperor is based on his own disdain for his predecessors and the Senate. He blames much of the Empire's recent decline on incompetent oligarchs and aristocrats whose ancient laws and traditions keep them in power by right of blood or wealth, rather than merit, intellect or ability. Despite this, though Garios is purportedly opposed to the oligarchic puppet Lucon and the aristocratic agitator Rhagaea, Garios supposedly governs his own third of the Empire by the force of his army, and is described as putting down opposition with his soldiers. Thus, Garios, separate from both Lucon and Rhagaea, who rely on tradition and inheritance respectively, believes in the law of might makes right as his basis for being Emperor.
Strengths and Weaknesses
Strength: Highly motivated veteran legions with populist reforms like land grants, fostering fierce loyalty and adaptability (e.g., quick adoption of crossbows), enabling aggressive offensives and high morale in the field. Garios is considered the best fighter, commander, and campaigner among the claimants, excelling in battlefield tactics and inspiring troops.
Weakness: Reliance on military might leads to authoritarian suppression of dissent, straining resources through constant rotations and risking overextension if veteran assemblies fracture under prolonged war.
Shared Military (Factional Variations)
All factions inherit the Thematic system (post-1080 AE under Arenicos, disbanding legions for levies), with rotations and land grants. Adaptations vary: Northern emphasizes Senate-funded mercenaries, Southern elite cataphracts, Western veteran cohorts. Strengths include administrative efficiency for standardization and quick adoption of enemy tactics (e.g., Vlandian crossbows, Nord Varangians, Steppe horse-archers).
