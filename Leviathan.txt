Leviathan
Colossal serpentine horrors stirred from abyssal depths by the Unveiling, leviathans measure hundreds of feet long, their horse-like or reptilian heads crowned with jagged teeth and armored scales that gleam like oil-slicked iron. They lurk at the map’s edges, feared as “creatures beyond the known,” coiling around ships with constricting force that crushes hulls like eggshells or biting clean through decks in a spray of splinters and blood. Storms herald their rare but catastrophic attacks—waves roil under false-moon light, survivors recalling glowing eyes like false stars and roils that swallow fleets whole. No confirmed slayings since the Unveiling; desperate crews fight with axes and spears in frenzied melees. Trophies adorn ships and halls: scales forged into unyielding shields, teeth carved into spearheads or amulets warding evil, hides tanned for sails that never tear. Materials are highly desirable—scales for alchemy-infused armor impervious to rust, teeth ground into powders granting visions or strength. None confirmed slain since the Unveiling’s height, they embody the ocean’s unknowable terror—a coiled shadow waiting for the storm to break.
