# Calradia Calendar & Date System
# Single source of truth for chronology, seasons, and campaign timekeeping
Era System

BE (Before Empire) — Years prior to the formal rise of the Calradic Empire.
AE (Anno Empire / After Empire) — 0 AE marks the year the Calradic Empire united many city-states and began its continental expansion.

Key historical anchor points:

Unveiling: 777 BE
Battle of Pendraic: Spring 1077 AE (March–May)
Current campaign date (as of last update): March 12, 1084 AE

Year Length & Structure

Year length: 365 days (standard solar calendar, no leap years unless narratively required for dramatic effect).
Months: 12 months mirroring the real-world Gregorian calendar:
January (31 days)
February (28 days)
March (31 days)
April (30 days)
May (31 days)
June (30 days)
July (31 days)
August (31 days)
September (30 days)
October (31 days)
November (30 days)
December (31 days)

Weeks: 7 days (naming optional; many cultures use descriptive terms like Market Day, Forge Day, Oath Day, Rest Day, etc.).
Seasons:
Spring: March–May
Summer: June–August
Autumn: September–November
Winter: December–February


Celestial & Seasonal Events

Solstices & Equinoxes (approximate dates, real-world equivalents):
Spring Equinox: ~March 20
Summer Solstice: ~June 21
Autumn Equinox: ~September 22
Winter Solstice: ~December 21

False Moon appearances: Random and unpredictable. Average ~once per month when charted from first recorded events (~777–780 BE), but intervals vary wildly (days to several months). Duration typically 1–4 nights; extremes recorded (e.g., 21 consecutive days in 800 AE).
Twin Moons: The natural celestial pair; predictable phases used for navigation, planting, and festivals.

Cultural & Religious Observances (Cross-Reference)

Druidic solstice/equinox rituals: March 20, June 21, September 22, December 21 (see Religions_Of_Calradia.txt for faith-specific details).
Aserai fasting months and pilgrimages: Tied to solar calendar.
Sturgian warrior feasts: Often around winter solstice.
Vlandian knightly tournaments: Spring and autumn (March–May, September–November).
Harvest festivals: Late summer/early autumn (August–October).
New Faith holy days: Aligned with solar events; Lycaron miracle commemorated ~three days after a major False Moon waning.

Campaign Date Tracking

Current in-game date (last updated): March 12, 1084 AE
All events, character ages, campaign progression, seasonal effects, and False Moon occurrences are tracked against this 365-day calendar.
GM note: When advancing time, use realistic travel durations (e.g., 20–40 km/day on good roads), seasonal weather impacts, and random False Moon rolls (e.g., 1–5% chance per night, or narrative trigger).
