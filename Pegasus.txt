Pegasus
High in Calradia’s most secluded alpine pastures—meadows cradled between snow-capped peaks and untouched by the smoke of villages or the tread of armies—Pegasus appear, rare and radiant as legends made flesh. Elegant winged horses with single spiraling horns rising from their brows, they embody purity and grace in every Calradian tale. Their coats are silver-white, shimmering like starlight caught in silk, manes and tails flowing as though stirred by an unseen wind even on still days. Hooves strike the earth without sound; wings, vast and feathered in soft iridescent hues, fold neatly against flanks when grounded, yet unfurl with breathtaking power in flight.
They only appeared after the Unveiling, one of the gentle wonders that slipped through when the veil thinned. Not all that crept into the world that night was foul—some gifts were luminous and kind. Pegasus are among them: fewer than thirty known to exist, glimpsed only by the fortunate or the pure. They shun humanity’s stain: the reek of iron, the clamor of forges, the blood of battlefields. They choose high, hidden places—valleys ringed by cliffs no ordinary horse could climb, pastures fed by glacial streams where no road leads. Here they graze on mountain flowers and drink from crystal springs, moving with an otherworldly serenity that calms even the wildest wind.
Their intelligence is profound—eyes deep and knowing, capable of understanding speech and intent far beyond any mortal steed. They bond only with the morally unblemished: knights of strict monastic orders like the Silver Spurs, hermits of unblemished heart, or wanderers whose souls remain untouched by cruelty or greed. The bond is absolute; a Pegasus grants its rider flight and subtle divine favor—swift passage over battlefields, protection from falls, an aura that steadies allies and unnerves foes. Failure to bond humbles the aspirant—often painfully, as the creature simply turns away, wings beating once in quiet dismissal.
To seek one is a pilgrimage of the spirit. Knights climb perilous heights, guided only by rumor and faith, hoping to prove themselves worthy. Those who succeed ride in legend; those who fail return changed—wiser, quieter, forever marked by grace they could not touch. In Vlandia, tales of Pegasus sightings inspire quiet quests and silent vows. They are not mounts to be claimed—they are mirrors, reflecting the rider’s true self. And in a world scarred by the Unveiling, few can bear that reflection.
