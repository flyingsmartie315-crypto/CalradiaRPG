Overview
The Gavys Riverlands is a broad, fertile river valley that forms the north-eastern heart of central Calradia. Dominated by the wide Gavys River and its tributaries the Zeos, Sethys, Aminys and Miron, the region is a mosaic of lush floodplains, rolling hills and ancient volcanic slopes that gradually climb into the Uchalion Massif and Mount Erithrys — Calradia’s tallest mountain. Long contested between marcher lords and lingering Imperial remnants (with Aserai influence pressing from the south across the Strait of Lysia and Battanian pressure from the north), the Riverlands remain entirely under Western Empire control. The mild climate and rich alluvial soil make it one of the most agriculturally productive regions in the Western Empire, yet its position as a major trade corridor keeps it a perpetual flashpoint of intrigue and skirmishes. The veil here is moderately thin — False Moon nights bring faint glowing mists along the rivers and occasional ghoul risings from old Imperial battlefields.

Settlements
Lageta (Town)
Lageta is a town of the Western Empire initially governed by the archon Crotor of the Dionicos clan. Lageta was infamously acquired by the Empire by an act of treachery. A rogue imperial mercenary murdered the Palaic chieftain who employed him, took the citadel, invited in imperial colonists, then bequeathed the town to the Empire. The Senate disavowed his action but accepted the bequest. The district is now thoroughly Calradicized, but the Battanians to the north remember, and whenever an imperial envoy speaks of the sanctity of treaties and pacts, they just nod to each other and say, "Lageta."

Villages
The villages of Montos and Primessos are bound to Lageta.

Montos: Sits at the base of the Cursion escarpment, near the Battanian frontier. The foresters here keep a watchful eye on their neighbors, always looking for raiding parties coming westward from the valley of the Miron river to their east.
Primessos: Lies in the wooded upper reaches of the Gavys valley. Pigs, fed on the acorns of the southern oak trees, thrive in this climate.

Hazards

Man-made: Border raids and political intrigue.

Hertogea Castle
Hertogea Castle is a castle of the Western Empire owned by Vipon of the Maneolis clan. Hertogea lies in the shadow of Mount Erithrys, a huge basalt slab thrust up from the depths of the earth. Villagers grow olives on the slopes leading up the mountain.

Gersegos Castle
Gersegos Castle is a castle in the Western Empire owned by Archon Saratis of clan Sorados. Gersegos sits in the gorges of the Miron river, as it cuts between the cliffs of the Cursion Escarpment and the Dryatic mountains. Villagers have sunk mines into a spur that overlooks the village, extracting silver and other precious metals.
