Full Armour Rules Summary
Below is a complete compilation of all armour rules established in our system for the Calradia RPG. This integrates tiers, AC values, coverage, layering, modularity, and resolution mechanics. All rules are designed to work with the core combat flow: single hit location per attack (reversed d100 roll), damage subtraction by type (Cut/Pierce/Blunt), and integration with DoS/DoF, AP actions, and weapon qualities (e.g., Armour Piercing reduces effective AC).
Overview
Armour Class (AC): Subtracts from incoming damage by type (Cut / Pierce / Blunt).
Coverage %: Chance (d100 ≤ %) the armour protects the hit location. Textile/Metallic Flexible = 100% on covered areas (facial/head max 80% for gaps). Rigid tiers have lower % due to joints/seams. Torso always 100% unless situational (e.g., unbuttoned –10%, hot day –5%).
Body Locations: Head, Right Arm, Left Arm, Torso, Right Leg, Left Leg (from hit location roll).
Tiers Layering: One piece per tier per location. Sum successful AC by type. Rigid cannot layer over rigid (clash/bulk). Mix lower tiers freely (e.g., gambeson under mail under plate).
Modularity (Rigid Tiers Only): Max 4 components per body part. Differential coverage (5–30% per piece based on size/function). Total coverage per part capped at 80% (gaps remain).
Encumbrance & Cost: Add all worn. Multi-location pieces scale +50%. Rigid Uniform lighter (–20% Enc) than Partial.
Attacker DoS Penalty: –10 to all coverage rolls per 2 DoS (cumulative).

Layering & Modularity Rules
Layering: Outer → inner resolution. One per tier per location. Rigid tiers cannot layer over each other on the same part (no plate over coat of plates).
Modularity (Rigid Tiers Only): Max 4 components per body part (e.g., arm: gauntlet + vambrace + couter + upper cannon). Differential coverage (5–30% per piece). Total cap 80% per part. Multi-location pieces (e.g., hauberk) have per-area % (torso 100%, arms 90%, legs 50%).
Rigid Construction Notes: Partial = many pieces (flexible seams, more gaps); Uniform = single/fewer pieces (rigid solid, fewer gaps but stiffer).
Coverage & Hit Resolution
Resolution Flow:
Determine single hit location (reversed d100).
Or a successful Called Shot hits a nominated location.
For the location, check each layer outer → inner: Roll d100 ≤ coverage % (adjusted by attacker DoS –10 per 2 DoS).
If succeeds, add its AC. If fails, skip to next (lower layers still apply if they succeed).
When an outer tier succeeds its AC coverage roll on a hit location, all lower/inner tiers automatically apply their full AC (no separate coverage roll required). If the outer tier fails its coverage roll, lower tiers still roll individually for their AC.
For modular rigid parts with multiple components: Roll d100 once for the part's total coverage (sum of pieces' %). If succeeds, roll die (d4 max) to randomly pick one successful component and use its AC.
Cascade: Upper failure doesn't negate lowers—always check all.
Updated Overlap Rule for AC on Same Location
For body locations with multiple overlapping pieces (e.g., long-sleeved gambeson and gloves both on Arms), after confirming the hit location (e.g., Arms), roll a single d100 to allocate the "sub-hit" based on each piece's coverage % of the location. Assign ranges proportionally (e.g., 1-90 for sleeve, 91-100 for glove). Apply only the AC of the "hit" piece. If percentages don't sum to 100% (gaps), uncovered ranges = 0 AC. This simulates precise strike location without violating "Head, Torso, Legs, Arms only" (sub-parts subsumed as proportional segments).
