Religions of Calradia
The Unveiling in 777 BE thinned the barriers between realities, allowing supernatural energies, creatures, and subtle manifestations to bleed into Calradia. This chaotic dimension—shaped by the conscious and subconscious thoughts, beliefs, and fears of Calradia's peoples—causes widely held deities and religious concepts to exert tangible, if often subtle, influence on the world. Miracles, omens, curses, blessings, prophetic dreams, and rare direct interventions become possible, especially under the False Moon, when the veil is thinnest and dark energies pool. Faiths gain renewed fervor or fracture as believers interpret Unveiling phenomena through their doctrines—some see divine judgment, others demonic incursion, and a few glimpse proof of their gods' living presence. Religions are not abstract philosophies but living forces that shape politics, warfare, culture, and individual destinies.
Major Religions and Sects
Calradic Faith – Old Pantheon (Traditional Imperial Rite)
The ancient polytheistic heart of the Calradic Empire before the schism. It venerates a hierarchy of gods under a supreme creator (the Light or the Maker), with lesser deities as patrons of war, trade, hearth, and state. Worship is public, ritualistic, and tied to the prosperity of the state. Temples are grand basilicas with altars for sacrifices; priests maintain the pax deorum (peace of the gods) through proper rites to ensure victory, good harvests, and stability.
Major Calradian Gods and Goddesses

Jovian — King of gods, god of sky and thunder, protector of the realm. Supreme ruler; patron of emperors, law, and oaths.
Junara — Queen of gods, goddess of marriage, childbirth, and women. Protector of families and imperial lineage.
Martius — God of war and agriculture, guardian of the Empire. Father of the Calradian people in myth; patron of soldiers and farmers.
Venara — Goddess of love, beauty, and fertility. Beloved of soldiers, merchants, and lovers.
Neptara — God of the sea, rivers, and earthquakes. Patron of sailors and coastal trade.
Minerva — Goddess of wisdom, strategy, and crafts. Patron of scholars, engineers, and tacticians.
Apollon — God of sun, music, archery, and prophecy. Bringer of light and plague; oracles interpret Unveiling omens.
Diana — Goddess of the hunt, wild animals, and the moon. Protector of forests and maidens.
Mercurius — Messenger god, god of commerce, travelers, and thieves. Patron of merchants and Freeblades.
Vulcanus — God of fire and the forge. Master craftsman; armourers offer tools at his altars.
Ceres — Goddess of agriculture and harvest. Ensures bountiful fields.
Vesta — Goddess of the hearth, home, and family. Eternal flame kept in every major temple.

Other Significant Deities
Pluton — God of the underworld and the dead.
Bacchus — God of wine, fertility, and revelry.
Janus — God of beginnings, endings, doorways, and transitions.
Saturn — God of time, agriculture, and the golden age.
Sol Invictus — The Unconquered Sun; popular in later Empire.
Mithras — Mystery god worshipped by soldiers.

Key Features
Syncretism: Absorbed foreign gods — Battanian spirits became woodland deities, Aserai sun-worship influenced Sol Invictus.
State Religion: Worship ensured state prosperity; emperors were divine intermediaries.
Ritual Focus: Correct sacrifice, augury, and pax deorum maintenance. No personal salvation doctrine.

Unveiling Influence: False moons as divine anger; veil bleed as monstrous punishment. Miracles claimed to reinforce clergy power.
Calradic Faith – New Faith (Ascendant Rite)
Monotheistic reformist sect worshiping a triune deity (Father, Son, Holy Spirit). Sees the Unveiling as apocalyptic revelation — a divine reckoning to purify the world. Miracles (healings, visions) are common claims; false moons are "holy trials."
Core Doctrine & Sacred Narrative

The Son was born to a mortal woman, revered as the Virgin Mother, who conceived through the Holy Spirit without earthly father. She is venerated as the perfect vessel of divine grace and intercessor for the faithful.
The Son lived among mortals, teaching love, justice, and sacrifice. He was betrayed, captured, publicly tortured, and executed by a corrupt regime during the 800 AE False Moon crisis in Lycaron. Through this suffering and death, the Father sacrificed part of Himself for humanity's redemption — atoning for sin and breaking the power of death.
Three days later, the Son arose again — alive, radiant, and unbroken. He remained in Calradia for 40 days, appearing to followers, strengthening the faithful, and commissioning them to spread the New Faith. At the end of this period, He ascended, promising to return at the final reckoning.
This miracle at Lycaron is the decisive founding event; the False Moon waned immediately after, absent for three years — the longest gap since the Unveiling.

Historical Precursor: Sol Invictus
Late-Imperial cults of Sol Invictus (the Unconquered Sun) had begun shifting toward monotheistic tendencies. Solar-centric theology laid the groundwork for the Trinity; the Lycaron resurrection was seen as the ultimate triumph of light over the False Moon's darkness.
Practices

Daily prayers facing the rising sun.
Fasting seasons commemorating temptation and post-resurrection periods.
Baptism symbolizing death/rebirth.
Communal meals echoing the last supper.
Pilgrimages to Lycaron and miracle sites.
Strict moral code: reject idolatry, embrace charity, defend the weak.

Organization
Hierarchical under the High Pontiff and bishops. Rapid spread post-800 AE; strong in towns, merchants, Vlandia, Empire splinters.
Unveiling Influence: False moons as tests of faith; veil bleed as demonic incursion; purification rites common.
Imperial Schism
Fractured between:

Old Pantheon — Polytheistic, ritual-heavy; strong among conservatives.
New Faith — Monotheistic, reformist; blames Unveiling on idolatry.
Schism fuels unrest: book burnings, relic hunts, kings playing both sides.

Battania Druidic Tradition
Ancient animistic faith of the forests. Worships the living world — sacred groves, ancient oaks, standing stones, river spirits, and the cycle of life/death. No single god; power resides in nature and unseen spirits.
Unveiling Influence: False Moon as balance disturbance; skinwalkers as corrupted entities; druids bind them. Dryads more active; mutants shunned.
Practices: Rituals at solstices/equinoxes, offerings to trees/stones, oaths on oak/iron, funeral pyres.
Sturgian Old Gods / Old Northern Faith
Polytheistic warrior faith centered on ancestor worship. Pantheon of semi-historical ascended heroes; primordial gods predate Unveiling.
Unveiling Influence: Monsters as twisted kin; direwolves sacred.
Practices: Blood-oaths, rune-carving, ship burials, shamanic trances.
Aserai Sun Faith
Monotheistic Eternal Sun (justice, life).
Unveiling Influence: Veil as djinn incursion; purification rites.
Practices: Daily prayers, fasting, pilgrimages.
Khuzait Sky Worship
Shamanistic reverence for Eternal Blue Sky, ancestors.
Unveiling Influence: Wisps as ancestors; false moons as anger.
Practices: Sky burials, horse sacrifices, star-reading.
Minor / Syncretic Faiths

Cult of the Unveiled — Worships Unveiling as revelation; small, persecuted mystics.
Gryphon Cults — Vlandian sects venerating gryphons as divine.
Dryad Reverence — Battanian spirit worship.
Ghoul & Drowner Taboos — Folk superstitions; burn dead to prevent rising.
