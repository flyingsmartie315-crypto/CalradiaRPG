Kraken
Enormous sea monsters released by the Unveiling, krakens lurk in Calradia’s deep gulfs and storm-tossed waters, their tentacles thicker than masts and tipped with barbed suckers that rend wood and flesh alike. Universally feared by sailors, they ensnare entire boats in a whirl of ink-black water, dragging vessels to the abyss amid shattering hulls and drowning screams. Survivors whisper of eyes like abyssal lanterns glowing in the depths, and a low rumble that shakes the waves before the strike. No known weakness holds firm—fleeing is the only sure escape, though desperate crews fight back with harpoons and fire-oil, aiming for the eyes or severing tentacles in frenzied melees. Trophies adorn ships and halls: scales forged into unyielding shields, teeth carved into spearheads or amulets warding evil, hides tanned for sails that never tear. Materials from these creatures are highly desirable—ink for potent poisons or indelible scripts, scales tougher than leather for exotic armor, tentacles dried into unbreakable whips. Yet most hunts end in disaster, the sea claiming the bold as readily as the timid. Krakens symbolize the ocean’s untamed wrath, a reminder that the Unveiling stirred things from the deep that no mortal can truly master.
