Ghosts
Since the Unveiling tore open the veil between realms, not every soul has found rest. Ghosts—specters, wraiths, lingering shades—remain trapped in Calradia by unfinished business, overwhelming trauma, or violent death that severed them from the afterlife. They haunt the places tied to their end: crumbling Imperial ruins, forgotten crypts, blood-soaked battlefields, abandoned villages where plague or massacre left no survivors. They are not uniform; each carries the echo of its own tragedy.
Most are malevolent or tragic—vengeful spirits driven by hate, pain, or betrayal. These attack the living: cold hands seize throats in the dark, whispers drive men to madness, apparitions replay the moment of death until witnesses collapse in terror. Others are bound—tethered to specific sites like graveyards, old gallows, or the room where they drew their last breath. These rarely leave their anchor but lash out at intruders or desecrators, manifesting as freezing touches, mournful sobs, or visions of their final moments.
Rarely are they purely evil in a demonic sense; most are harmful remnants of tragedy—echoes that must be put to rest through resolution (righting an old wrong, returning a stolen item) or exorcism by clergy wielding holy rites and blessed iron. Curses trap many: oaths sworn in blood, forbidden rituals, or dying words that bind the soul to endless repetition.
The Unveiling thinned the veil; false-moon nights amplify their presence. Manifestations grow vivid—cold that seeps into bones, whispers that speak forgotten names, apparitions solid enough to touch. Some ghosts aid the living—guiding lost children home, warning of danger—but most curse or torment. They are common across Calradia, thickest in old Imperial battlegrounds and ruined fortresses where thousands died unburied.
To the living they are a reminder: death does not always end suffering. A chill wind through an empty hall, a shadow that lingers too long, a voice calling from the next room—these are not always tricks of the mind. In Calradia, the dead sometimes refuse to stay silent, and the living must learn to listen—or pay the price.
