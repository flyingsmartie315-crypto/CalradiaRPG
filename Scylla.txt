Scylla
Smaller kin to the colossal krakens, scylla emerge from the Unveiling's shadows in coastal shallows and reefs, their tentacles slimmer but no less deadly—coiling like whips to snag lone swimmers or small boats. Feared by fishermen and coastal traders, they strike with sudden ink clouds and barbed grasps, pulling prey below amid churning foam and muffled cries. Eyes glow faintly in murky waters, a rumble like distant thunder preceding attacks. Weaker than krakens, they can be repelled with spears or fire; crews target the central body or sever limbs in close-quarters fights. Trophies are more common: smaller scales for light armor, teeth for daggers warding minor evils, hides for durable ropes. Materials valued—ink for dyes or poisons, suckers preserved as hooks. Hunts are risky but feasible in groups; scylla represent the sea's lurking peril, a constant threat to shores where the deep meets the land.
