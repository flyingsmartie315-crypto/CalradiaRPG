Battanian Culture Framework
Overview
The Battanians are the ancient woodland folk of Calradia — the original inhabitants of much of the continent, now reduced to a fiercely defiant pocket of free territory in the northwest. They are masters of the longbow, the night raid, and the sudden wild charge out of the woods. Battanian culture idolises valour, but especially when mixed with a sharp sense of mischief: the cattle thief who can vanish with an entire herd into the fog, or the champion who dines with a rival tribe only to later produce from his bag the skull of one of their kinsmen as a battle souvenir.
Ruled (in name) by High King Caladog, the Battanians occupy the heavily forested highlands of the Uchalion Plateau.
Geography & Territory (Uchalion Plateau)
The Uchalion Plateau is the densest and most ancient forest region on Calradia. These rugged misty highlands feature steep valleys, ancient oak groves, and near-impenetrable woodland. The veil between worlds is dangerously thin here, making the land both spiritually potent and highly dangerous. Natural barriers, man-made defences (hilltop fortresses, hidden trails, deadfall traps, and camouflaged ditches), and unnatural phenomena (whispering mists, spectral lights, and creatures that slip through the veil) combine to make the Plateau incredibly difficult to invade.
The forests yield rich resources: game, wild honey, medicinal herbs, rare yew wood for longbows, and excellent timber. Rivers and hidden tracks serve as the main trade (and raiding) routes.
History
From time immemorial there has been a High King of Battania, crowned with great ceremony on the sacred hill of Dunthanach. Yet ask any chieftain whose “kingdom” he lives in and he will gesture to his hall, fields, pastures, flock, and retainers and reply “Mine, of course.” Only lately, following the example of the Empire, have the High Kings made any serious effort to exert real authority. One recent High King remarked, “Such unruly cattle as my people require a strong herdsman’s hand to steer them from the ravine and protect them from the wolf,” moments before he was betrayed by a jealous cousin and taken in chains to the nearest Imperial outpost.
Battanian clans once populated most of the continent. The expanding Calradic Empire gradually pushed them back until only the Uchalion Plateau remained free. Their hilltop fortresses have borne witness to countless wars against imperial legions, and more recently against the rising Sturgian and Vlandian kingdoms. The Battanians have never been fully conquered — they simply melt into the trees and strike from the shadows.
Society & Government
Battania is a loose confederation of fiercely independent clans. Each chieftain rules his own hall, fields, and warband with near-total autonomy. The High King’s authority is more ceremonial and symbolic than absolute, though Caladog has begun trying to weld the clans into a more cohesive force. Blood feuds, cattle raids, shifting alliances, and personal reputation dominate politics. Honour and clever cunning matter as much as martial skill. Bards and druids preserve oral law, history, and spiritual traditions.
Culture, Language & Settlements
Battanian communities feature distinctive roundhouses, hillforts, carved timber halls, ringforts, and sacred standing stones. Many settlements are deliberately hidden deep in the forest or built atop defensible hills.
Intricate spiral and knotwork patterns decorate weapons, shields, torcs, and jewellery. Warriors often wear dyed cloaks or protective runes. Battanian speech and personal names carry a distinctive flavour unique to the clans.
Military
Battanians excel at asymmetric woodland warfare.

Core troops: Elite longbowmen capable of devastating volleys from cover, light skirmishers, and wild-charging warriors armed with falxes, spears, and round shields.
Tactics: Night raids, ambushes from the treeline, hit-and-run strikes, and sudden charges that exploit the dense forest and thin veil for supernatural confusion. They avoid pitched battles in open ground.

Unveiling Influence
Nowhere is the veil thinner than on the Uchalion Plateau. On False Moon nights the forests come alive with spectral lights, whispering voices, and otherworldly creatures. Battanian druids and hedge-witches harness these energies for rituals, curses, protective charms, and moon-touched arrowheads.
Current Situation (March 1084 AE)
As the Calradic Empire tears itself apart in civil war, High King Caladog senses an opportunity to reclaim lost ancestral lands. Yet the clans remain as unruly and independent as ever. Sturgian raiders probe the northern borders while Vlandian knights eye the western woods. The misty highlands of the Uchalion Plateau stand ancient, watchful, and ready to swallow any army foolish enough to march beneath their boughs.
