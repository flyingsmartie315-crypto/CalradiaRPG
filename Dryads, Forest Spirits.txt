Dryads, Forest Spirits
Dryads, ethereal spirits born from the Unveiling's veil-thinning, embody the ancient heart of Calradia's wild woodlands. They manifest as subtle presences: a whisper in the wind carrying warnings, branches twisting to form barriers or paths, or faint humanoid forms woven from bark, leaves, and vines—eyes like glowing sap, hair flowing as mossy tendrils. Non-aggressive by nature, they judge intruders with unerring insight: the pure-hearted receive guidance, such as vines parting to reveal hidden trails or illusions dispelling dangers ahead. The corrupt or destructive face hindrance—misdirecting fogs, entangling roots, or deceptive echoes leading to peril.
Common in Battania's dense Rhennod thickets and Rhennod forests, they are rarer in cleared lands, fading where axes and fires scar the earth. Druids and Fian warriors revere them as allies, offering rituals of respect to invoke aid. False moons amplify their power, making groves shimmer with otherworldly energy. Encounters are treacherous for the unworthy, a reminder that the forest lives—and remembers.
