Overview
The Khuzaits are the horse lords of the eastern grasslands and steppes — a proud people who have transformed from free-roaming nomads into the rulers of a vast and restless khanate. Once content to raid and trade with the Empire before vanishing back into the wilds, they were forced into unity two generations ago by threats from the distant east. They are renowned for their unmatched horsemanship, lightning mobility, and remarkable skill at governing conquered territories that contain many different cultures and faiths. The symbol of the Khuzait Khanate is the nine-horsetail banner.
Ruled by Monchug Khan, the Khuzaits occupy the eastern grasslands and steppes of Calradia.
Geography & Economy
The Khuzait lands stretch across the vast open steppes known as the Great Grass Sea, east of the former imperial heartlands. These endless rolling plains provide perfect grazing for enormous herds of horses, sheep, cattle, and mules. The economy is built on livestock and trade in hides, wool, meat, and fine war-mounts. Khuzait lords possess generations of nomadic herding knowledge; when a village or region falls under their rule, production of sheep, cows, mules, and horses rises by a full quarter compared to lands governed by outsiders. This superior animal husbandry — rooted in expert breeding, seasonal migration patterns, and masterful grazing management — sustains both thriving markets and the enormous remount needs of their all-mounted armies.
History
For centuries the steppe tribes lived as nomads, venturing into imperial lands to raid and trade before returning to their ancestral freedom. The disunited clans (Khuzait, Nachaghan, Arkit, Khergit, and Karakhergit) were kept divided by the Empire through bribes and political maneuvering.
Two generations ago, powerful forces from far to the east pushed westward, threatening the border tribes. Under the leadership of Urkhun the Khuzait, the clans united into a confederacy, swept into the poorly defended eastern provinces of the Empire, and overran them. They levied taxes and tariffs on captured towns and villages, establishing the Khuzait Khanate. Urkhun imposed strict discipline, requiring the clans to ride to war only at his command instead of whenever they wished.
After Urkhun’s death the spirit of unity weakened. His descendants continue to rule the khanate, but many clans chafe under central authority. Some dream of claiming the khanship for themselves, while others resent the gradual shift from pure nomadic freedom toward a more settled existence. The once-loose confederacy has become a true khanate, yet the old independent spirit still burns strongly.
Society & Government
The Khuzait Khanate blends nomadic tradition with settled rule. Monchug Khan sits at the head of a hierarchy of loyal khans and noyans who command both mobile warbands and fixed territories. While some unruly clans (particularly the Karakhergit) resent the loss of complete freedom, the khanate has proven remarkably effective at administering conquered lands. Khuzait governors excel at managing territories inhabited by multiple cultures and religions, allowing local customs to continue while maintaining overall control, steady taxation, and justice. This pragmatic approach has helped stabilize their holdings in the former eastern Empire.
Military
Khuzait armies are uniquely and entirely mounted. Even the lowliest common tribesman rides to war with his own steed — often two or more horses for long campaigns, remounts, and pack animals. This all-cavalry composition grants unmatched mobility and endurance, allowing devastating horse-archer volleys, hit-and-run raids, feigned retreats, and rapid encirclement of enemy forces across the open steppe.
Unveiling Influence
The vast steppes experience the False Moon differently than forested or mountainous regions. On nights when the second moon rises, the endless grass can appear to move like a silver ocean. Some Khuzait shamans read omens in the shifting patterns of the grass under moonlight, while others claim the open sky makes the veil feel thinner and the stars more watchful.
Current Situation (March 1084 AE)
With the Calradic Empire fractured by civil war, the Khuzait Khanate stands in a position of growing strength. Monchug Khan must balance the desire of many clans to raid deeper into the weakened imperial lands with the need to maintain order and administer their existing territories. Internal tensions between traditional nomadic clans and those who have embraced settled rule continue to simmer beneath the nine-horsetail banner.
