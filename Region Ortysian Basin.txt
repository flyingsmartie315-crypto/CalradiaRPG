Overview
The Ortysian Basin is a broad, fertile lowland valley. Carved by the Gavys, Sethys, Aminys and Zeos rivers and fed by volcanic ash from the western caldera, it is a patchwork of golden grain fields, olive terraces, and gentle rolling hills that gradually rise into the mist-shrouded Epiric Hills, Mount Epiric, and the Epiric Eastern Pass to the north-west, while the Glen of Balasog and the Cafalcomb mark its northern edge. Long contested between Vlandian marcher lords and lingering Imperial remnants (Aserai from the South across the strait and Battania from the North), the basin is primarily Imperial held. Vlandia’s only holding in the north-west of the region is the important port of Charas. This mixed occupation creates a complex landscape of trade, intrigue, and occasional border skirmishes. The veil here is moderately thin — False Moon nights bring only a gentle hush and faint glowing mists over the rivers, but ghouls occasionally rise from old Imperial battlefields and skinwalkers have been sighted in the deeper wooded pockets. The basin’s mild climate and rich soil make it one of the most prosperous regions in the Western Empire, yet its divided loyalties keep it a perpetual flashpoint.

Settlements
Charas (Port City)
Charas is a town of the Kingdom of Vlandia ruled by Baron Ingalther of Clan dey Cortain. The city known today as Charas is traditionally reckoned to be the first Calradian colony on the shores of their new continent. At the Empire’s height, when the capital moved north to Paravenos, Charasea remained a key hub of sea-faring and trade, while imperial aristocrats built their summer homes on the warm shores of the nearby gulf and cruised their pleasure-boats around its islands. When the Vlandians came, Charas fell into the hands of the ruthless House of dey Cortain, and its wealth funds their ambitions.

Ortysia (Port City – Larger Imperial City)
Ortysia, named for the quails who inhabit the nearby coastal marshes, was the first Calradian colony on the shores of the Perassic Sea. In its early centuries, it fell under the authority of the Kannic city-state of Quyaz, but the Empire’s expansion soon united the colony with its mother republic. For centuries, sailors coming from the pirate-infested islands of the Gulf of Charas gave thanks when they saw its towers looming through the haze of the western Perassic, signalling a return to safe imperial-patrolled waters. Southern cultural influence from the Aserai sultanate remains strong in Ortysia’s mercantile quarters, visible in the colourful awnings, spice-scented markets, and dhow-style vessels that mingle with Vlandian carracks in the harbor.

Villages
The villages of Arpotis, Vinela, and Garengolia are bound to the town.

Arpotis: Sits on a high bluff overlooking the confluences of the Aminys, Gavys and Zeos rivers. The sides of the hills are terraced to grow wheat, with cisterns to catch the rain blowing up from the sea.
Vinela: Sits beneath the waterfalls where the Aminys river rushes down to join the Perassic Sea. Villagers collect salt from the sea in the shadow of coastal cliffs.
Garengolia: Lies near a rich vein of silver in the Pangaion Hills overlooking the Gavys river. The Empire have minted its denars from these mines for centuries.

Thractorae Castle
Thractorae Castle is a castle of the Western Empire owned by Crotor of the Dionicos clan. It sits on a small bay that feeds into the larger Gulf of Charas. These lands, stirred by hot winds from the south and watered by hillside springs, have been used by imperial landowners for the experimental growing of date palms.

Garontor Castle
Garontor Castle is a castle of the Western Empire owned by Apys of the Varros clan. It gives its name to the promontory between the Gulf of Charas and the inland Perassic Sea. Coastal meadows, where tall grasses tolerant of the ocean salt-spray thrive, support the rearing of fine horses.

Veron Castle
Veron Castle sits at the southern entrance of the Battanian highlands and is overlord to the villages Veron and Goleryn. It is believed that Veron was the birthplace of Emperor Turiados. The castle is owned by clan Corenios of the Western Empire.
