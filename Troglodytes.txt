Troglodytes
In the high, cold reaches of Calradia’s mountains—caverns of Mount Jacul, abandoned mines, and wind-scoured ridges—the troglodytes have adapted as a stable subhuman branch from Unveiling's warping mutations, honed by natural selection in the depths. Bodies are smaller than surface kin to fit confined spaces—rarely taller than 5'6", compact and wiry with elongated limbs for squeezing through narrow fissures. Skin is pale and almost translucent, eyes small and near-blind from generations in absolute darkness; they navigate by a crude form of echolocation—sharp clicks and low hums that bounce off stone, allowing them to map tunnels and detect movement with eerie precision.
These traits breed true through generations: offspring inherit the compact frame, translucent skin, and echolocation instinct, maturing in lightless chambers where they learn to “see” by sound alone. Clans form rigid hierarchies—the strongest claim the deepest tunnels, shamans read omens in dripping stalactites and echoing drips. They raid nocturnally, stealing food and captives from mountain villages, retreating before dawn with their prizes. Mutations favor survival in low-oxygen depths: larger lung capacity, dense bones that resist crushing rockfalls, claws that dig through stone. They use crude stone and flint tools—sharpened flakes for cutting, chipped hammers for breaking rock—or whatever they can steal from villages (knives, picks, torches). Grim ornaments decorate lairs: bone fetishes, stolen iron rings strung on sinew, cave walls engraved with crude spirals and claw-mark runes—half-remembered symbols of a lost humanity. False-moon nights rouse entire clans—clicks rising to howls—as they surge to the surface in search of prey.
Villagers burn cave mouths when discovered, knowing the mutants will tunnel new exits if one is sealed. To those who live in the heights, every sudden sinkhole or missing goat carries the same fear: the mountain itself has learned to breathe and hunger. These clans are reclusive but vicious when cornered—proof that the Unveiling gave the stone new children, and their blood runs cold and deep.
