Availability
Having the money doesn’t always guarantee the desired good or service is at hand. Hamlets and small villages are far less likely to have expensive goods than a large city of 10,000 people. Moreover, finding a high-quality sword in a backwater village is far more difficult than locating such a weapon in a teeming metropolis. To reflect availability, each good and service has an associated availability ranging from Very Rare to Abundant. These notations are a base availability and should serve as a starting point for determining whether a town has an item or not. For example, Middenheim is a coastal city and, naturally, fish would be more common there than in a place like Karak Kadrin, situated as it is in the World’s Edge Mountains. Hence, feel free to adjust the levels of goods and services to suit the location of your game.
Characters searching for a particular item or service must make a successful Gossip Skill Test modified by the availability of the item.

Use Gossip or Haggle tests to find higher-quality items; DoS can reduce search time or unlock rare craftsmen. In Calradia, Unveiling-touched forges or dryad-blessed herbs may grant automatic “Best” quality at extra risk (e.g., +1 IP on failure).

Craftsmanship
Not all goods and services are created equal. A rusty sword from a back-alley smith is very different from a masterwork blade forged under a dryad’s moon or in a veiled dwarven forge. Use the table below to adjust cost and availability when characters seek (or craft) higher/lower quality items.
Craftsmanship Table
Craftsmanship,Cost Multiplier,Availability Change
Best,×10,Drop 2 steps
Good,×3,Drop 1 step
Common,×1,No change
Poor,×½,Increase 1 step
Examples

A Common steel longsword in a medium town: 50 sc, Routine Gossip test.
A Good version of the same sword: 150 sc, Average Gossip test.
A Best masterwork blade: 500 sc, Hard Gossip test (or drop to Scarce if the town is small).

Characters with Trade (appropriate speciality) can craft items at one level higher than their workshop/materials would normally allow on a successful test with DoS ≥3.
