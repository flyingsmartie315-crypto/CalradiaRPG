Goblins
Goblins slunk into Calradia through the Unveiling’s cracks—post-cataclysm pests, humanoid but twisted, hunched and scuttling like rats in man-form. Standing 3 to 5 feet in their perpetual slouch, they are diminutive next to humans, legs short and stumpy for awkward bursts of speed, but arms long and disproportionately strong, dangling nearly to the ground. This lopsided build makes them clumsy in open sprints but deadly in bursts: they hurl stones, javelins, or crude darts with shocking force and accuracy, their favored throwing weapons suiting their wiry upper strength perfectly. Greenish-brown skin, beady black eyes, wide nostrils, and jagged teeth complete the grotesque picture—faces forever sneering, as if the world is one big joke at humanity’s expense.
Primitive scavengers, they use tools fashioned from bone, flint, or stolen scrap—far inferior to human ironwork. No forges or fine smiths among them; their spears are crooked branches tipped with sharpened stone, clubs just heavy sticks wrapped in sinew. This technological gap is their greatest weakness: in fair fights against armed men, they shatter like dry twigs. They know it too—goblins never fight fair. Stealthy as shadows, they rely on traps: pit-falls lined with stakes, silk trip-lines (from herded spiders), or poison-smeared thorns hidden in undergrowth. Ambush is their creed; they strike from cover, swarm the fallen, then melt away before reinforcements arrive.
Intuitive cunning defines them—good at reading terrain, weather, and weakness, but not deep thinkers. Tribes form loose, squabbling packs of up to 200, led by the biggest bully or slyest trickster, dwelling in shallow burrows, stolen ruins, or spider-infested caves. Talented at animal husbandry, they tame non-intelligent Unveiling creatures—especially spiders—as mounts, herders, or war-beasts. A goblin riding a burrower spider through steppe grass or leading a pack of web-spinners in forest raids is a sight to chill the blood.
Widespread and adaptive, goblin tribes infest every corner: desert burrowers with sand-camouflaged hides, steppe nomads on spider-mounts chasing caravans, northern fjord-dwellers huddling in ice caves with furred variants. They raid isolated humans opportunistically—lone farms, unguarded wagons, stragglers from armies—competing with other Unveiling horrors for territory and scraps. False-moon nights frenzy them: tribes unite in chittering hordes, traps multiply, and raids turn bolder under the sickly glow.
To humans they are vermin—cullable with fire and steel—but their cunning makes extermination hard. A goblin never meets you head-on; it waits in the dark, trap set, dart poisoned, giggling softly as you step wrong. In Calradia’s wilds, every rustle or distant cackle warns: the small things are watching, and they play dirty.
