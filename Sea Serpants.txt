Sea Serpents
Smaller relatives of the leviathans, sea serpents slither through coastal currents and reefs, their scaled bodies (50-100 feet) armored but agile, heads snapping with venomous fangs to strike at boats or swimmers. Common in trade lanes, they surface during storms to coil and crush smaller vessels, eyes glowing faintly as waves churn. Survivable with harpoons or fire; crews aim for the eyes or underbelly in desperate battles. Trophies more attainable: scales for light shields, fangs for poisoned daggers, hides for flexible sails. Materials prized—venom for alchemy, bones for amulets. Hunts are organized by guilds; sea serpents represent the everyday peril of the waves, a constant reminder that the Unveiling's gifts lurk just below the surface.
