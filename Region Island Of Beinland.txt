Overview
The Island of Beinland is a remote northern isle in the Western Ocean, marked by volcanic lowlands, frozen straits, and walrus beaches, serving as a hardy outpost for Sturgian and Nord settlers amid eternal winters and Unveiling mysteries. Bordering the Sea of Ghosts to the north and Byalic Sea to the east, it acts as a whaling and ivory trade hub, with clans defending against raiders and sea horrors. False moons awaken auroras and unnatural ripples, while economies rely on fisheries, grain from volcanic soil, and walrus hunts. Settlements endure isolation, blending seafaring rites with vigilant defenses against the encroaching cold.

Settlements
Hvalvik (Port Town)
Clustered longhouses and whaling docks hug the Bay of Hvalvik's southern straits, where harpoons gleam under auroral skies amid volcanic ash fields and migratory whale paths. Markets trade ivory and blubber, saga chants echoing over geothermal springs as clans muster against northern raiders. False moons ripple bays unnaturally, countered by ritual fires and sea wards.
Location and Ties:
Geographic Structure Link: Southern Straits (South), Bay Of Hvalvik (South)
Neighbouring Features: Sheltered harbors with black sands and hot springs; ash-enriched meadows for hardy crops.
Kingdom/Clan Affiliation: Sturgian/Nord hybrid (ruled by local jarls under Vlandian influence), focused on whaling and northern trade.

Lagshofn: Founded by explorer Lag following sea pillars; descendants sail straits between Hvalvik and Vlandia to hunt whales, blackfish, and migratory quarry between Byalic Sea and Sea of Ghosts.
