Ruling Clans of the Empire (Western)
Comnos
The populist military house that currently controls the western third of the former Calradic Empire, with key holdings in Jalmarys (City), Zeonica (Port City), and Onica Castle. Champions of the legions over the Senate and aristocracy, the Comnos clan rose to power through the sword and the voice of the common soldier. They represent a long-standing populist current in imperial politics, demanding veteran land grants and decision-making by military assembly rather than ancient bloodlines or wealth. While they criticize the old oligarchic system, they maintain control through the loyalty of their veteran legions.
Emperor Garios Comnos — Mid-forties, broad-shouldered and battle-scarred, with close-cropped hair and a steady gaze that silences rooms. Wears practical lorica over tunic, sword always at side. Charismatic voice rallies troops; generous to allies, devious in plots, daring in field, cruel to foes, calculating in court. Once a legion soldier, rose to general insisting army chooses rulers. Proclaimed Emperor by veterans after Arenicos' murder, contesting Lucon and Rhagaea. Claims uncle Pateor beheaded by Lucon for veteran petitions. Loyal to friends but manipulative; rules with populist assemblies of soldiers, shutting opposition.
Politics
Garios is the face of the populist faction. He has long argued that the army, not incompetent aristocrats or the Senate, should choose the rulers of the Empire. He pushed for major reforms including veteran land grants and popular trial by military assembly. These ideas were repeatedly blocked by the Senate and aristocratic interests, leading him to believe the old system is rotten beyond repair.
Claim to the Throne
Garios openly scorns the old imperial order. He blames the Empire’s decline on oligarchs and aristocrats who cling to power through blood and wealth rather than merit. While he criticizes Lucon’s oligarchic puppetry and Rhagaea’s aristocratic entitlement, Garios himself rules his western third largely by the strength of his army — a practical application of “might makes right.” He maintains that true legitimacy comes from the will of the soldiers who actually defend the realm.
Vendelia Comnos (born 1037 AE) — Noblewoman of clan Comnos, wife of Emperor Garios, mother of Nadea. 47 years old, poised and elegant with sharp features and flowing dark hair. Wears fine silks in imperial purple. Just and honest in judgments, but ungrateful to benefactors; navigates court with quiet integrity amid populist chaos.
Nadea Comnos (born 1060 AE) — Noblewoman of clan Comnos, daughter of Garios and Vendelia, sister of Thephilos. 24 years old, athletic and vibrant with bright eyes and braided hair. Wears practical riding attire. Brave in battle, generous to allies, a loyal friend; embodies the empire's hopeful youth amid civil strife.
Thephilos Comnos (born 1064 AE) — Nobleman of clan Comnos, son of Garios, brother of Nadea. 20 years old, lean and intense with a piercing stare. Wears armored tunic. Ruthless in pursuits, willing to crush obstacles; a product of wartime ambition, he bears no physical resemblance to his sister Nadea, and once beat a man to death for implying infidelity on the part of the Western Emperor.
