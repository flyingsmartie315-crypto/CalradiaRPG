Talents are exceptional edges, specialized training, innate gifts, or Unveiling-touched abilities that set a character apart from ordinary folk. They enhance Characteristics, Skills, or open new options without replacing core mechanics.
Acquisition

New characters begin with 2 Talents (chosen during creation or rolled randomly).
Additional Talents gained through milestones, training, Unveiling events, or rare rewards.
Maximum: 8 (risk of strain or madness beyond this).

Talent List
Acute Hearing
Your hearing is exceptionally sharp.
Gain a +20 bonus to any Characteristic test (Awareness, Perception, etc.) or derived Skill test that relies on hearing (eavesdropping, detecting approaching footsteps, noticing subtle sounds in the dark, etc.).
Alley Cat
You are completely at home in the twisting streets, back alleys, and rooftops of towns and cities.
Gain a +10 bonus to Concealment and Silent Move tests in urban environments (streets, slums, rooftops, sewers, crowded markets, etc.).
Ambidextrous
You can use either hand with equal skill and precision.
You do not suffer the usual -20 penalty when using a weapon, tool, or shield in your secondary/off-hand. (Applies to dual-wielding, off-hand parries, throwing with either hand, etc.)
Artistic
You possess genuine creative talent and an eye for beauty.
Gain a +20 bonus to relevant Trade skill tests when creating art, illustrations, sculptures, illuminations, or similar works.
Additionally, gain a +10 bonus to Evaluate tests when appraising objects of art, jewelry, fine craftsmanship, or decorative value.
Contortionist
You can bend, twist, and manipulate your body in ways that seem almost unnatural.
Gain a +10 bonus to relevant Performer (acrobatics, contortion displays) tests.
Gain a +20 bonus to Agility tests (or derivative Skill tests) made to escape bonds, squeeze through tight spaces, dislocate joints to escape manacles, contort through bars, or perform similar feats of extreme flexibility.
Coolheaded
Your mind remains calm even in the face of terror or chaos.
You gain a permanent +5 to your Willpower (WP) Characteristic (this may increase your Willpower Bonus if it crosses a tens digit).
Dealmaker
You have an instinctive feel for value and negotiation.
Gain a +10 bonus to Evaluate and Haggle tests (treated as +10% on the d100 roll).
Disarm
You are skilled at stripping weapons from your foe’s grasp.
When you successfully strike with a melee attack, you may choose to attempt a disarm instead of inflicting damage. Make an opposed Weapon Skill (WS) test.

If you win, your opponent is disarmed and their weapon drops to the ground (it may be recovered with a Ready action).
If your opponent wins, they retain their weapon.
Natural weapons (claws, teeth, tentacles, etc.) cannot be disarmed.

Etiquette
You are well versed in the social graces, customs, and subtle hierarchies of the upper class.
Gain a +10 bonus to Charm and Gossip tests when dealing with Nobility (lords, ladies, barons, courtiers, knights of high station, etc.).
This bonus also applies in other situations where knowing proper social etiquette is useful, such as:

Disguise tests when impersonating a nobleman, courtier, or high-born individual
Avoiding social blunders in noble households, formal banquets, or diplomatic settings
Navigating court intrigue, receiving invitations, or presenting yourself credibly among the elite

Excellent Vision
Your eyes are keen and piercing.
Gain a +10 bonus to any Characteristic test (Awareness, Perception, etc.) or derived Skill test that relies on sight (spotting distant movement, reading faint tracks, noticing hidden details, shooting at long range, etc.).
Fearless
You are either brave or mad enough that you know no fear.
You are immune to Fear and treat Terror as ordinary Fear (i.e. you still roll against Terror but do not suffer the worse effects of true Terror).
You are also immune to the effects of the Intimidate skill and the Unsettling talent (or similar fear/aura effects).
Flee
When your life is on the line, you are capable of impressive feats of speed.
When running away from combat or another dire threat, you gain a +1 bonus to your Movement characteristic for 1d10 rounds. (This bonus applies only to fleeing actions and ends once the immediate threat is escaped or combat ends.)
Fleet Footed
You are naturally swift of foot.
You gain a permanent +1 bonus to your Movement characteristic.
Frenzy
You can incite yourself into a frothing, berserk rage.
You must spend 1 full round psyching yourself up (howling, biting your shield, beating your chest, etc.).
The next round you go berserk: gain +10 to Strength, Toughness, and Willpower, but suffer -10 to Perception, Weapon Skill, and Intelligence.
You must attack the nearest enemy in melee combat; all attacks must be All-Out Attacks, Charge Attacks, or Swift Attacks.
You may not flee or retreat unless you pass a Challenging (+0) Willpower test.
Otherwise you remain in this state until the combat is over (or you are incapacitated).
Frightening
You have a truly frightening appearance or aura.
You cause Fear in others (opponents must pass a Fear test to engage you in combat or approach you willingly).
(This talent may be taken by mutants, beastmen, scarred veterans, or those touched by the Unveiling.)
Grudge (Various)
You harbour a deep, burning hatred for a specific group or entity (e.g. Aserai, Goblins, Vlandians, Wælhreów, mutants, etc.).
You gain a +5 bonus to Weapon Skill (WS) and Ballistic Skill (BS) tests made against members of that group.
(Note: Your roleplay should reflect this aversion, even if it is detrimental in specific circumstances — refusing parley, prioritizing them as targets, etc.)
Lightning Reflexes
Your reactions are lightning-fast.
You gain a permanent +5 bonus to your Agility characteristic (this may increase your Agility Bonus if it crosses a tens digit).
Linguist
You have a natural affinity for languages.
Gain a +10 bonus to all Read/Write and Speak Language skill tests (including learning new languages faster or deciphering unfamiliar dialects).
Lucky
At the most improbable times, things seem to go your way.
You gain +1 Fortune Point each day (resetting at dawn or after a long rest).
(Fortune Points may be spent to reroll failed tests, gain extra Degrees of Success, or trigger narrative boons — see Fortune rules if implemented.)

New Talent: Weapon Specialization (Category)
This talent replaces the old flat “Specialist Weapon Group” and reflects real progression from formal training to hard-won battlefield experience.
You may take this talent once per weapon category. Each time you take it you choose one of the following categories and one of the three levels below.
