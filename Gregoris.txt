Brother Gregoris the Blessed
Title: Veteran Exorcista of the Order of the Silvered Star
Common Names: Gregor, the Hexer, Old Gregor, the Mule-Borne Saint
Age: Approximately 150 years (appears mid-40s due to divine blessing)
Height: 6'10"
Build: Towering, massively muscled, yet surprisingly agile and quick for his size. Bald, heavily scarred, with a perpetual grim expression that makes most people take an involuntary step back.
Appearance & Attire
Gregoris generally adorns himself in a long, plain monk’s habit of coarse dark wool that completely conceals the valuable equipment strapped directly to his body. Beneath the habit he wears reinforced steel plate and mail of dark, unadorned steel over practical leather and heavy wool. A staggering number of blades (longsword, multiple throwing knives, daggers) are strapped across his chest and belt, but more often than not when trouble finds him he resolves it without drawing a blade. He carries a simple-looking but extremely valuable staff on his long walks — carved from the ivory of a leviathan, pale and faintly luminous, etched with subtle protective runes. The only visible symbol of the Order is a simple silver sunburst pendant. His exact outer attire is subject to daily variation depending on the road and the threat, but the monk habit always remains to help him escape notice and maintain a low profile.
Mount & Travel
Due to the Order’s strict vow of poverty, Gregoris walks the roads of Calradia more often than not. He leads a sturdy pack mule laden with seemingly mundane items — old blankets, patched sacks, simple tools, and other things people wouldn’t think to steal. The only real valuables on the mule are basic provisions and traveling necessities.
A Pegasus (divine mount of the Silvered Star) only appears when the situation is truly dire — a sign from the gods that the Unveiling threat has grown beyond what one mortal man should face alone.
Background & Personality
A “blessed” mutant born under a particularly violent False Moon. The mutations granted him superhuman strength, speed, agility, and dramatically slowed aging — blessings the Order officially recognises as divine favour.
He has spent over a century as an Exorcista, hunting the most sophisticated and dangerous Unveiling abominations. Quiet, laconic, and world-weary, he speaks little but observes everything. Deeply faithful to the Order’s tenets, yet pragmatic enough to work with outsiders (including Sigismund) when the threat demands it. He carries the weight of 150 years of horrors with stoic resolve and the occasional dark humour.
Role in Calradia
The man the crown, the Church, and desperate villages call when the Unveiling spawns something that thinks. He is the specialist who arrives quietly, solves the problem with silver, steel, ritual — or, more often than not, with a single swing of his leviathan-ivory staff — then disappears down the road with his mule before anyone can offer him more than a hot meal and a blessing.
