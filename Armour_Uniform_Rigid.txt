Rigid Uniform Interpretation Rules
For Rigid Uniform pieces, parse the item title for module and material (Wrought Iron, Low-Carbon Steel, or Hardened Steel) to determine base AC, Enc, and coverage.
All Rigid Uniform modules have a hard cap of 90% per location due to joints and articulation.
Coverage is built from modular components tied to location tags (additive).
AC applies proportionally. Roll d100 ≤ coverage % on hit to apply AC; failure = gap.
Base Enc from module; material modifiers added separately. Round to nearest 5.
Mandatory Under-Layers: Any Rigid Uniform on arms requires Arming Doublet; on legs requires Padded Hose (otherwise -10 Agility and -1 AC from chafing).
Upgrades from the Universal Armour Upgrade Components Table may be applied if the material matches.
Craftsmanship global (+1/+2 AC); inherent rigidity gives no Dodge bonus.

Rigid Uniform Helmet Rules
(Most Up-to-Date Version – 2026-03-19)
Core Construction
Rigid Uniform helmets are built as monolithic or near-monolithic solid-plate pieces with minimal seams and joints. This gives them superior structural integrity and durability compared with Rigid Partial helmets, but they are slightly heavier and less flexible.
Base Coverage
Base coverage percentage varies by helmet model (see individual model values in the table below). The 60/30/10 internal split is used only as a reference for upgrade module calculations:

Skull + Cheek Guards: 60% Head
Neck area: 30%
Face area: 10%

Maximum Coverage Rule
No Rigid Uniform helmet may exceed 95% total coverage. The final 5% is always the visor zone, which remains 0 AC unless a visor upgrade is fitted.
Automatic Visor Rule
Visor-specific cultural tags are applied only when the Full Visor upgrade module (or specific visor variant) is present.
The narrator will then automatically describe the exact visor style matching the tag and only on the models listed below:
