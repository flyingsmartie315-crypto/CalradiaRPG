Overview
The Gulf of Charas is a vast southern oceanic basin of warm turquoise waters, coral reefs, and storm-tossed straits, serving as a crucial maritime trade artery between Vlandian coasts and Aserai ports while harboring Unveiling sea horrors in its depths. Bordering Neurval and Swadia to the north and the Perassic Sea to the south, it blends Vlandian shipping with Aserai influences, defending against pirates and krakens. False moons stir abyssal rumbles and glowing eyes below. Economies rely on fisheries, spices, and gulf trade, with ports thriving amid islands and inlets—a realm of abundance where naval ambition meets the deep's mysterious warmth.

Galend (Port)
Grand Vlandian port in the far northwest gulf, with soaring spires and tourney grounds amid olive groves and aqueducts. Royal seat of King Derthert, blending feasts and imperial renewal. False moons whisper from ruins below.
Location and Ties:
Geographic Structure Link: Biscan Coast, Neurval Inlets
Neighbouring Features: Rugged cliffs with pebble beaches; sheltered bays with tidal flats.
Kingdom/Clan Affiliation: Vlandian (ruled by King Derthert of House dey Meroc as the royal seat), focused on gulf defenses and trade.
Structure and Layout:
Core: High keep with waterfront guildhouses and tourney fields.
Key Districts: Royal palace for feasts; dockyards for river galleys; cathedral for rites.
Defenses: Broad walls with harbor gates; watchtowers on cliffs; naval patrols in inlets.
Population and Daily Life:
Size and Density: Large port (6,000-12,000 residents), densely urban along quays with rural olive edges.
Demographics:
Garrison: Knights for charges; crossbowmen on walls; scouts on cliffs.
Civilians: Merchants in markets; farmers in groves; women in guilds.
Economy: Olive oil exports; ironwork; gulf and river trade.
Culture and Customs: Lavish jousts and banquets; cathedral rites; aqueduct festivals.
Hazards:
Man-made: Baron rivalries; smuggling in inlets.
Bestiary: Drowners in tidal flats; mire-kin along shores.
Unveiling: Whispers from imperial ruins; false-moon shadows.

Scyllian Chain (Misc Outpost)
Chain of eight small forts across the Octa-Scylla Archipelago, each perched on rocky isles amid deep coves and turbulent waters. Vlandian garrisons combat piracy, but most islands serve as refuges for rampant buccaneers. Signal fires link the chain, watching for incursions amid shipwrecks.
Location and Ties:
Geographic Structure Link: Octa-Scylla Archipelago
Neighbouring Features: Rocky isles with sea caves and reefs; pirate havens in shallow depths.
Kingdom/Clan Affiliation: Vlandian (contested garrisons amid pirate strongholds), focused on anti-piracy watches and smuggling control.
Structure and Layout:
Core: Small stone forts with signal towers, surrounded by cove docks and reef barriers.
Key Districts: Watchtower quarters for signals; harbor posts for patrols; cave storages for supplies.
Defenses: Island isolation with reef hazards; tower emplacements for fire signals; small naval skiffs.
Population and Daily Life:
Size and Density: Small outpost chain (100-300 per fort, total 800-2,400), sparse on isles with clustered garrisons.
Demographics:
Garrison: Vlandian marines and archers; scouts on reefs.
Civilians: Smugglers and refugees; fishermen in coves; women in support roles.
Economy: Limited trade tolls; scavenging from wrecks; piracy spoils.
Culture and Customs: Tense watches and signals; uneasy truces with pirates; sea wards.
Hazards:
Man-made: Pirate incursions and betrayals; smuggling rivalries.
Bestiary: Sea serpents in coves; krakens near wrecks.
Unveiling: Glowing eyes during false moons; abyssal rumbles.
Vikhertai Towers (Misc Outpost)
Nine small garrisons dotting the Vikhertai Chain, overseeing the eastern edge of the Gulf and Strait of Lysia. Contested between Western Empire and Aserai Sultanate, towers watch for naval threats amid volcanic isles. Signal chains link them, guarding shallow depths.
